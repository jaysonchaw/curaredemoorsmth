# Teacher Onboarding Guide

Welcome to Curare! This guide will help you get started with creating classes, publishing assignments, grading student work, and managing lessons.

## Getting Started

### 1. Create Your Account

1. Go to the teacher signup page
2. Enter your full name, email, and password
3. Enter your **Teacher Code** (provided by your institution)
4. Verify your email address
5. Log in to your dashboard

### 2. Create Your First Classroom

1. From your dashboard, click **"Create Classroom"**
2. Fill in:
   - **Name**: e.g., "Biology 101 - Fall 2024"
   - **Description**: Brief description of the class
   - **Grade/Age Range**: e.g., "Grade 9" or "14-15 years"
3. Click **"Create Classroom"**
4. **Copy the 7-character join code** (e.g., `O7DCUIK`)
5. Share this code with your students

**Students join by:**
- Going to the student access page
- Entering the classroom code
- Completing their profile

## Managing Lessons

### Map Lessons to Your Classroom

1. Open your classroom
2. Click the **"Lessons"** tab
3. You'll see two sections:
   - **Classroom Sequence**: Lessons currently mapped to your class
   - **Available Lessons**: All lessons from the global roadmap

### Add Lessons to Your Classroom

1. Find a lesson in the **"Available Lessons"** section
2. Click **"Add to Class"**
3. The lesson appears in your **"Classroom Sequence"**

### Organize Your Lesson Sequence

1. In **"Classroom Sequence"**, drag and drop lessons to reorder them
2. Students will see lessons in this order

## Creating Assignments

### Step 1: Create Assignment

1. Open your classroom
2. Click the **"Assignments"** tab
3. Click **"Create Assignment"**

### Step 2: Fill in Basic Details

- **Title**: e.g., "Cell Biology Quiz"
- **Description**: Instructions for students
- **Due Date**: When the assignment is due
- **Visibility Window**: When students can see/submit (optional)

### Step 3: Add Questions

Click a question type button to add questions:

- **Multiple Choice**: Students select from options (can have multiple correct answers)
- **Fill in the Blank**: Students type a short answer (pattern matching)
- **Free Text**: Students write a longer response (manual grading)
- **Open Response**: Long-form essay questions
- **Sequence**: Ordering questions
- **Timed Task**: Questions with time limits

### For Each Question:

1. **Question Body**: Write your question
2. **Points**: How many points this question is worth
3. **Hint** (optional): Provide a hint for students
4. **Adaptive-enabled**: Check if this question can use adaptive feedback

### For Multiple Choice Questions:

1. Add options (minimum 2)
2. Check the box next to correct answer(s)
3. You can have multiple correct answers

### For Fill in the Blank:

1. Enter the correct answer pattern (regex)
2. Add synonyms that are also acceptable

### Step 4: Save or Publish

- **Save Draft**: Save without publishing (students won't see it)
- **Publish**: Make it visible to students (they'll get a notification)

## Grading Submissions

### View Submissions

1. Open your classroom
2. Click the **"Assignments"** tab
3. Find the assignment
4. Click **"View"** to see submissions

### Filter Submissions

- **By Student Name**: Search for a specific student
- **By Status**: Show only graded or ungraded submissions

### Auto-Grade Multiple Choice

1. Click **"Autograde MCQs"** button
2. All multiple choice questions are automatically graded
3. Free text questions still need manual grading

### Grade a Submission

1. Click **"Grade"** next to a submission
2. Review each question and student answer
3. For multiple choice: See if answer is correct/incorrect
4. Enter the **Score** (out of total points)
5. Add **Comments** for feedback
6. Click **"Submit Grade"**

### Export Grades

1. Click **"Export CSV"** button
2. A CSV file downloads with:
   - Student names and emails
   - Scores
   - Grading timestamps
   - Teacher comments

## Creating Posts

### Create an Announcement

1. Open your classroom
2. Click the **"Posts"** tab
3. Click **"Create Post"**
4. Write your message
5. Options:
   - **Pin to top**: Keep this post at the top
   - **Visibility**: Class only or teachers only
6. Click **"Post"**

### Manage Posts

- **Pin/Unpin**: Click the pin icon to pin important posts
- **Delete**: Remove posts you no longer need
- **Search**: Use the search bar to find specific posts

### Comments

- Students and teachers can comment on posts
- Teachers can delete inappropriate comments
- Click **"Show Comments"** to view/add comments

## Tips for Success

### Organizing Your Classroom

1. **Start with a Welcome Post**: Pin a welcome message with class expectations
2. **Map Lessons in Order**: Organize lessons in the sequence you want students to follow
3. **Lock Lessons Initially**: Unlock lessons as you progress through the curriculum

### Creating Effective Assignments

1. **Mix Question Types**: Use multiple choice for quick checks, free text for deeper understanding
2. **Set Clear Due Dates**: Give students enough time but maintain deadlines
3. **Use Hints**: Help struggling students without giving away answers
4. **Start with Drafts**: Create assignments as drafts, review, then publish

### Grading Efficiently

1. **Use Auto-Grade**: Let the system grade multiple choice automatically
2. **Grade in Batches**: Grade all submissions for one assignment before moving to the next
3. **Provide Feedback**: Use comments to help students improve
4. **Export Regularly**: Keep backups of your gradebook

### Engaging Students

1. **Post Regularly**: Share updates, reminders, and encouragement
2. **Respond to Comments**: Engage with student questions and discussions
3. **Pin Important Info**: Keep key information (syllabus, due dates) pinned
